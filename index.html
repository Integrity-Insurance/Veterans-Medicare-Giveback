Part B Premium Reduction



Some Medicare Advantage (MAPD) plans offer a Part B Premium Reduction—sometimes called a "Giveback".

Annual Enrolment Period (AEP) for those plans October 15th - December 7



How It Works:


Instead of paying the full standard Medicare Part B premium of $185.00, the plan may refund a portion of that amount, effectively increasing the monthly Social Security benefit you receive.





How Medicare Advantage Works with VA Benefits




Veterans who have Medicare (Part A and Part B) are eligible to enroll in a Medicare Advantage Prescription Drug (MAPD) plan for the same reason as any other Medicare beneficiary:




As long as they are enrolled in both Part A and Part B and live in the plan’s service area, they can legally enroll in an MAPD plan.


Having VA benefits does not disqualify them from Medicare Advantage — the programs are separate and can work together.


VA Health Care only covers care received at VA facilities or through VA-authorized providers.


Medicare Advantage (MAPD) plans provide coverage through civilian (non-VA) doctors and hospitals, giving veterans access to a broader network.


MAPD Plans often include extra benefits the VA doesn’t cover, such as:



  • Dental coverage

  • Vision care

  • Hearing aids

  • Over-the-counter allowances

  • Free complimentary gym memberships


  • Grocery card (Prepaid VISA)

  • Flex Card Benefits (Prepaid VISA)
